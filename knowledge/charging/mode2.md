# Mode 2 Charging (IEC 61851‑1)

## Definition
Connection of an EV to a standard household socket (single or three‑phase) using a **charging cable with in‑cable control and protection device (IC‑CPD)**.

## How it works
1. User plugs IC‑CPD into socket.
2. IC‑CPD performs self‑check (ground, leakage sensor, over‑temp).
3. User connects Type 2 plug to vehicle.
4. Vehicle and IC‑CPD communicate via control pilot (CP) signal.
5. Charging starts after safety checks and pilot handshake.
6. During charging, IC‑CPD continuously monitors leakage, temperature, and ground.

## Typical parameters
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Voltage | 230 V AC (single‑phase) |
| Current | 6 A, 8 A, 10 A, 13 A, 16 A (adjustable) |
| Power | 1.4 kW – 3.7 kW |
| Cable length | 5 m – 10 m |

## Advantages
- Portable, no installation.
- Works with any Schuko or heavy‑duty socket.

## Disadvantages
- Slow charging (full EV may take 8‑10 hours).
- Safety depends heavily on IC‑CPD quality.

## For Zinova Nomad
- Must have **plug temperature sensor** (socket quality in Iran is unreliable).
- Must have **over‑current derating** when plug overheats.
