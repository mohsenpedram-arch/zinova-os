# IEC 61851-1 – Electric vehicle conductive charging system

## Scope
Specifies general requirements for off-board and on-board EV charging equipment for AC and DC charging, up to 1000 V AC and 1500 V DC.

## Relevance to Zinova Nomad
Defines the four charging modes. Nomad is a **Mode 2** charger. Key requirements:
- Control pilot (CP) circuit for safety and communication.
- Pilot signal: 1 kHz PWM, duty cycle indicates maximum current.
- Vehicle must confirm before current flows.

## Clauses particularly important for IC‑CPD
- **Clause 8**: Protection against electric shock (requires RCD, ground monitoring).
- **Clause 9**: Control pilot function.
- **Clause 12**: Temperature monitoring requirements.
- **Annex A**: Pilot signal waveforms.

## Relation to IEC 62752
IEC 62752 is the **product standard for Mode 2 IC‑CPD** and references IEC 61851‑1 for general principles. Any IC‑CPD must comply with both.

## Red flags for Zinova Agent
- Vendor claims “compliant with IEC 61851” but cannot explain CP signal.
- No ground continuity detection.
- No pilot signal generation.
