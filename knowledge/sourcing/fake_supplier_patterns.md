# Fake Supplier Patterns and Scams

## Common fraud types in EV charging sourcing

### 1. Fake material scam
- Sample uses genuine components (e.g., copper wire, rated relays).
- Mass production uses inferior materials (aluminium, iron with copper coating).
- **Recent case**: Buyer paid $50,000 for copper plates, received iron slabs coated with copper.

### 2. Bait‑and‑switch
- Perfect sample (often produced at a loss).
- After large order, manufacturer switches to cheaper parts, lower‑grade plastics, substandard PCB.
- **Prevention**: Hold 20‑30% payment until independent pre‑shipment inspection (PSI).

### 3. Brand impersonation
- Scammer creates fake Alibaba profile using name, logo, photos of a legitimate brand.
- Offers products at 40% discount.
- **Check**: Contact the real brand directly via their official website.

### 4. Ghosting after deposit
- Supplier disappears after receiving 30‑50% deposit.
- **Prevention**: Use Alibaba Trade Assurance or escrow service. Never wire 100% upfront.

## Red flags in communication
- No video call possible (always excuses).
- “Our factory is in another city, can’t visit”.
- Provides only mobile number, no landline or company email (@gmail.com, @qq.com).
- Pushes for full payment before shipment.
- No business license provided.

## Required verifications for Zinova
1. **Live video call** showing the production line and QC area.
2. **IEC 62752 certificate** with test report (verify certificate number with issuing lab).
3. **Factory business license** (check address on Baidu Maps).
4. **Sample order** with full testing before mass production.
5. **Pre‑shipment inspection** by third party (e.g., SGS, Bureau Veritas).

## If any of these is missing → **do not proceed**
