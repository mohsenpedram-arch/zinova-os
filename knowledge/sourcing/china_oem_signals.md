# China Sourcing – How to identify a real OEM

## Positive signals (increase score)

| Signal | Why it's good |
|--------|----------------|
| Business license shows “manufacturing” (制造) or “production” (生产) | Legally allowed to produce |
| Factory address in industrial zone (e.g., Bao’an, Dongguan) | Likely owns production line |
| Company can issue 17% VAT invoice | Only real manufacturers can |
| Technical discussion includes “tooling”, “mould”, “PCB layers” | Has engineering depth |
| Offers to sign NDA and share schematics | Transparent and confident |
| Production line video (timelapse or live) | Real proof |
| Previous export to EU or Middle East | Passed certification audits |

## Negative signals (penalise or reject)

| Signal | Why it's bad |
|--------|---------------|
| Name contains “Trading”, “Import/Export”, “Sourcing” | Not a factory |
| Address is a commercial building (e.g., Room 301, Building B) | No factory floor |
| Cannot provide factory registration number | Hiding real production |
| Sales rep avoids engineering questions | No technical capability |
| Refuses video call or factory walkthrough | Probably a trader |
| Price is 30‑50% lower than average | Classic bait‑and‑switch |

## Hybrid suppliers
Many Chinese companies are both factory and trader (produce some products, trade others). **Always verify** that the specific product (IC‑CPD) is made in their own factory, not outsourced.

## Recommended questions for WeChat/WhatsApp
1. “Can you show me your IC‑CPD production line?”
2. “What is your monthly output of IC‑CPD?”
3. “Do you design the PCB or buy it from a third party?”
4. “Can you provide a factory audit report?”
